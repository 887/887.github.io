About

30 Apr 2023

Why 887? It’s a prime number and has 3 letters. That’s it. No deeper meaning behind it. I can type it on my keyboard hitting the keys really fast. That’s why.

Hi, i’m a software dev. Most of the software that i ended up writing over the decades has been for my employer or personal projects. So you can’t find my projects here on github. That’s ‘what happened’. I do use and support open source. I’ll employ open solutions when possible. I’m ‘classically’ (closed source) employed and i’m i’ve been coding for two decades. As far as licenses go i’ll abide by what is agreed upon. If i were to license things i want them to succeed. Sometimes that means money, sometimes that means wide spread adoption. I read the news and a lot of people in tech just lost their job. Even more automation is coming and machine learning is a thing. My guess is things will just change like always and i’ll have to adapt, like always. The quality of my work keeps me employed and life is at least in some part about ‘survival’ when it comes to work. So far i’m actually doing ok on that part. My identity on that comes down to this: Given the nature of reality i’ll develop solutions in exchange for something of value, given that i like the idea or more likely, you pay me and I use what ever tools we agree on. Let’s hope we do get personal assistant humanoid robots one day to fold the laundry and clean the bathroom and they are open source so they don’t stab us, or at least not without it getting fixed later. My ideal world is one of open source but software devs still getting compensated for their work. I’m more pragmatical rather than idealistic, but I do like things to run on linux and having most of my tools open.

As to doing work. Money can’t help me focusing or comprehending a problem and you can pay me lots to keep me motivated, but it requires a human element and a dialog on top most of the time. I’m open to ideas. I’m open to discussion. I like to listen, I like civil arguments, I like to learn. The experiences we make shape us. Some make us forgive more and be better people, others make us hate wasting our time and stress us out. Usually then something will have to give or change, otherwise people walk away. I generally like to be in a position where it’s not ‘an emergency’ and things can be done right. And on the opposite i don’t like endless meetings where nothing actionable ever comes out.
I tend to focus better when something ‘feels’ right, so me having the right feeling about something is actually the best course of action to keep me happy and productive. I try to give the right feedback and ‘feeling’ back and i’m guessing that’s what the nature of the way i conduct business in reality looks like. So as to writing software i’ll freely admit i had a massive ego, was not doing my homework and thinking ‘i-can-do-it’ when i should have been humble. These days i believe in what i develop, lead and try to get along depending on what is required. If i can’t believe in something, i’ll reach a compromise or move on. I learned that if you communicate with little ego and with careful respect for the ego on both ends, don’t project any bad intension, put the effort in and believe in people, most things do turn out with both parties ending up in a healthy working relationship. Game theory, psychology, philosophy are all now part of my thoughts. Empathy is knowing that you never know what the other party is really thinking or feeling and not trying to hurt their feelings. Cooperation wins in the end if things are done right and there is a common goal.

I recommend this little game: https://www.clearerthinking.org/post/evolution-of-trust-game

What else.. I enjoy linux, automation and automated solutions in general, play factorio and games like it. Since so much I do all day involved sitting i’ve picked up exercise a few years ago to compensate.

So i don’t want to overshare and get on with my projects.

Cat’s or dogs? Both.
