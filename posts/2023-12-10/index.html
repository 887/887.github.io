Almost healthy again

10 Dec 2023

What happened:

So in October my manager noticed i was yellow in a work meeting. I didn’t really notice it at home because i’m using the ‘warm’-yellow lamps and i only felt the pain from it for few days, a week before and took some Ibuprofen which made it go away.

It first was suspected to be hepatitis, but i was negative on all types and it turned out to be Gallstones.

I spent a week in two different hospitals, because they didn’t have the tool to break up the stone in the first one, and i was on heavy Antibiotics because my liver wasn’t working. I got all my vaccine shots refreshed in September so i didn’t catch anything. üê±

I got my gallbladder removed and now i’m just waiting on getting a final endoscopy to get the last stent out, than i’m back to full health.

The gallbladder was inflamed and there was still a fragment in the gallcanal which they didn’t read in the paperwork. Operation was supposed to be an hour but took longer. üòº

By my count i could have died almost 3 times, maybe more.

The doctors and nurses all showed of how things work and taught their students or junior doctors how to do their job and i received comments on how good my health is otherwise. It’s comforting because they all didn’t seem to concerned about me and used me as an example for how a patient should look like. I can’t buy health for that but since i work on software i like it when things are easily identifiable. It’s not exactly the same, but i felt the healthcare system was working for me and if i didn’t take care of my health i would have had way more complications and died. One doctor that asked about my history told me to stay-the-course. That’s what i had in mind and now it’s doctors orders. üòπ

I lost a lot of weight years ago and worked out a lot since then.
Looks like having been overweight caught up with me eventually though.

My personal doctor called my journey through this an Odyssey and way harder than it should have been because there were lots of complications and i was at the doctors every few days. I didn’t comment because this isn’t how i think. I did everything under my control the best i could. I worked this journey like i work on code. You just do one step after the other and do the best you can each step. Complaints about the process get you nowhere. I answered all the questions, was friendly and worked this out. I got apologies for mistakes made at steps by the doctors. I felt like everyone was doing their best and i didn’t even know they did something wrong until they apologized, when they made mistakes. I can’t expect them to work perfect, i can only try to be my best and i felt like i was. It wasn’t really an Odyssey where i wanted to give up at any point because I made my choice to live and so i work the situation.

The first few weeks with this i spent working on my project, when i still could, but the operation for the removal threw me out of my routine. The pain and a big hematoma bulb from the operation, as well as what the doctors told me meant i couldn’t do sports and lift heavy things until things are healed. So i spent time healing and recovering. Just doing the little things around the house carefully was enough. üò∫

I spent this week coding a lot and did ’light’ workout. Building back my strength. I’m slowly getting back to heavier workouts.

I have no complications from having no gallblader anymore and can still eat what i want.

I remember a time when i was younger. There this would have thrown me out and made me change my life. But now.. it wasn’t life changing. Like i got the introspective look on my life when i was in pain, but compared to what i got when i was younger my mind went more along these lines: Well, i’m paying for what we broke figuring myself out. The course we are on isn’t bad though, the world tells me i did good. All i can do i’m doing, to survive this. This is paying a fee for not always being on this course, and that’s ok. Same as sometimes you have good karma you also get bad karma from things you did in the past.

I was roommates with other patients in the hospital that were there because of their choices in life too. A few of them actively self-destroying, others trying to get more years out of life.

A lot of time for self reflection, but no big revelations. Huh. It was a good experience in my book.

I got lots more planned in the coming months and i have project plans and spreadsheets on what i do want to do.

tl;dr; I went through a lot. I took care of my health. Now i can continue on. Life is good. No impact on my long-term-health. üëç
